About Navy Corpsman Training

Navy Corpsmen complete a rigorous five-month medical course covering the fundamentals of hospital care. Afterward, they advance into either hospital residency training or a two-month field medical course, where they gain the discipline, endurance, and leadership required to operate alongside United States Marines.

Once trained, Corpsmen are assigned to Marine units, where they continue to train and teach Tactical Combat Casualty Care (TCCC), preparing Marines to perform life-saving procedures under combat conditions.
