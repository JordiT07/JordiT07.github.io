Website Evaluations

Below are two websites I evaluated using the CRAP design principles: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity.

Apple

Apple’s official site is sleek, simple, and well-organized, showcasing strong visual hierarchy and clean design.

  • Contrast: Excellent use of light and dark tones to emphasize visuals and buttons.
  • Repetition: Consistent typography and layout across pages.
  • Alignment: Perfectly aligned elements make the content easy to follow.
  • Proximity: Related items are grouped logically, improving readability.

Toronto Cupcake

Toronto Cupcake’s site has a fun concept, but the design is cluttered with inconsistent spacing, busy backgrounds, and small text, making navigation harder than it should be.

  • Contrast: Limited; some text blends into backgrounds and images, reducing readability.
  • Repetition: Inconsistent use of fonts and colors, which makes the site feel less cohesive.
  • Alignment: Some elements are misaligned or unevenly spaced, which interrupts visual flow.
  • Proximity: Related items are sometimes too far apart, and unrelated elements appear too close, making it harder to find information quickly.
